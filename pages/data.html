Our Data Sources

All visualizations on The Unheard Earth are built from credible, peer-reviewed scientific data. We prioritize transparency and accuracy, sourcing information from leading research institutions, government agencies, and international scientific organizations.

Explore the datasets below to understand the sources behind our stories and access the raw data for your own research and analysis.

Trusted Data Partners

We source our data from the world's leading scientific institutions:

NASA

Goddard Institute for Space Studies (GISS) temperature data and satellite observations

NOAA

National Centers for Environmental Information climate records and ocean data

NSIDC

National Snow and Ice Data Center for polar ice measurements and analysis

IPCC

Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change assessment reports and datasets

World Bank

Global environmental indicators and socioeconomic climate impact data

IUCN

International Union for Conservation of Nature biodiversity assessments

Access & Usage

All data presented on this site is publicly available and sourced from open scientific databases. We encourage researchers, educators, and concerned citizens to explore these datasets and draw their own insights.

For questions about data methodology, processing, or visualization techniques, please refer to our documentation or reach out to our team.
